Adversarial Samples from "Adversarial Attack on GMM i-vector based Speaker Verification Systems"

  • Authors: Xu Li, Jinghua Zhong, Xixin Wu, Jianwei Yu, Xunying Liu and Helen Meng
  • Abstract: This work investigates the vulnerability of gaussian mixture model (GMM) i-vector based speaker verification (SV) systems to adversarial attacks, and also the attack transferability across features or/and model architectures (x-vector based systems). In detail, we formulate the GMM i-vector based system as a scoring function, and leverage the fast gradient sign method (FSGM) to generate adversarial examples through this function for attack. These adversarial examples are used to perform both white and black box attacks. We measure the vulnerability of the systems by the degradation of their equal error rate and false acceptance rate. Experimental results show that GMM i-vector based systems are seriously vulnerable to adversarial attacks, and these attacks are proved to be transferable and pose threats to neural network speaker embedding based systems (e.g. x-vector systems).
